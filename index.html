Rev. Mrs. Lucy Perbi Nyarko

21st November, 1958 - 2025

Also Known As: Lucy Osei Afari

The late Rev. Mrs. Lucy Perbi Nyarko was born on 21st November, 1958 to the late Opanin Kwaku Nyamekye from the Nsoatre Nsesreso family and the late Obaapanin Grace Osei Afari from the Sunyani Gyeduakro family. She started her elementary school at the Nyamaa Basic School in 1964 but completed at the Sunyani Anglican school in 1974. When she was in middle form 3, she passed the Common Entrance examination to go to Ghana Secondary School in Koforidua but her late grandfather Opanin Adolphus Osei Afari who took care of her education declined to send her there due to her petite structure. Unperturbed by that decision, she again sat for the examination when she got to middle form four (F4) and passed to go to Sunyani Secondary School with the ambition to become a medical doctor.

Unfortunate for her, her grandfather who preferred Technical Education to the Grammar type, decided to send her to the Sunyani Technical Institute (Sunyani Technical University) to do Catering for two years and end up as a Matron. Again, due to her structure the then principal, Mr. F. A. Baiden advised the grandfather to let her do Business which was four years with the hope that her physical structure would improve after the four years.

Education Journey

From the Sunyani Technical Institute, she proceeded to Takoradi Polytechnic in 1978 with her two certificate which were obtained concurrently i.e. Royal Society of Arts (RSA) and General Certificate of Education (G. C. E 'O' level) to do her Diploma in Business Studies (DBS) Accounting option and completed in 1981.

Aided by Mr. Philip Awuah who bought forms and also vacated his room for her at campus, she was encouraged to attend the University of Cape Coast in 1982, to pursue the Bachelor of Commerce (B. Com), Accounting option and graduated with B.Com Hons. Degree and Diploma in Education simultaneously in 1987, due to the one-year University closure by the P. N. D. C Administration.

After school, she was posted to her Alma Mata, Sunyani Technical Institute to do her National Service, where she taught accounting. Imbued with the Spirit of sacrifice, she at that time, organized special 'A' level Accounting Classes at the Twene Amanfo Secondary School for some students, some of which have graduated in B.Com and are in Administrative positions not forgetting the numerous female students who have completed their Masters, Degrees and Nursing programmes. To her "I cannot do Spirit" was an affront to her dignity and alien to her upbringing.

Professional Career

After completing her National Service Programme she was employed at the Ghana Water Company in 1989 as Officer. Impressed by her prowess in accounting she was tasked to assist the audit unit in the Commercial company. In order to keep her and make her feel comfortable, an estate house was bought for her to stay in.

In 1995, the Volta River Authority (VRA) needed some Finance Officers and so she applied, attended interview and was employed and sent to the Tamale Area office to work as Area Finance officer. She was transferred back to Sunyani as Area Finance Officer and back to Tamale head office and back again to Sunyani still as Area Finance officer. Here in Sunyani, she got the opportunity to act as Area Manager. She later attended an interview for managerial position and she was subsequently promoted to the position of Area Manager breaking the long standing monopolized administrative structure. To be precise, she was the first female to rise to that position. Again, she was the first person from non-engineering background to get to that position thanks to her hard work, tactfulness, dexterity and acumen.

Further Education & Community Impact

While in active service, she got the opportunity in 2003 to travel to the United Kingdom to study at Leicester University where she obtained her Master's Degree in Business Administration (Finance option). She formed the VRA Ladies Association and encouraged many cashiers to further their studies some of whom have become finance officers. She organized field trips and excursions for the ladies taking them to places like Burkina Faso and Dubai. Through her instrumentality, some females from poor houses were identified and adopted by the Ladies Association and their educational expenses were catered for by the association.

From Sunyani, she was transferred to Bolgatanga as Area Manager. Her impact over there, mesmerized the workers and the community to the extent that the Bongo Chief enskinned her as Development Queen of Bongo and given the title Timnalana. She was so dear to them that when she retired in 2018, a convoy was sent to bring her back to Bolgatanga to continue her good works there not to mention the educational opportunities initiated for the youth and the donation of items to the needy and elderly.

Family Life

To the family, Rev. Mrs. Lucy Perbi Nyarko was a pivot around which the care and support of her siblings revolved. She was an oasis of strength, direction and financial support a vacuum which will be difficult to fill. She was respectful, co-operative, task oriented, serviceable, frank and out-spoken.

Back in September 1985, Rev. Mrs. Lucy Perbi Nyarko married to Mr. Crosby Perbi Nyarko. They had plans to celebrate their fortieth (40) year marriage vows this year only to be taken aback by the unexpected news from Accra where she received medical care.

Christian Life & Ministry

Madam Lucy's Christian life has been quite chequered, starting at the Methodist Church in her childhood days to the Assemblies of God Church (AG), Calvary Charismatic Centre (C.C.C) and Elim City where she was one of the founding members, culminating in her being ordained as a minister of the gospel in 2016.

At Elim City, she was a board member, was in charge of church finances, Bible School Teacher (Life in the word), Marriage Counsellor (Family life), women ministry organizer and Pastor in charge of the Twi section (Aseda Congregation).

Post-Retirement Activities

After retirement from active service, Rev. Mrs. Lucy Perbi Nyarko continued to offer her skills in support of humanity. She was an executive members of Sunyani Technical Institute Alumni, Regional Executive member of Ghana LPG Operators Association (GLIPGOA) and the National Treasurer. She was also the treasurer of the VRA Retirees Association.

The entire family, numerous people you have been supporting and those still under the care of the Perbi Nyarko family will forever miss you.

Rest well in the bosom of Abraham. Shalom.

TRIBUTE BY DAUGHTER

"Those who live in the shelter of the Most High will find rest in the shadow of the Almighty." — Psalm 91:1 (NLT)

I want to thank you all for coming to celebrate the life of my mum with us today.

Maa, this was your favourite bible verse. You loved the Lord and loved spending time in his presence. Never did you miss a church gathering or anything related to God's work. You raised us on God's word. Each morning at 5:30am without fail, you will come knocking on our doors for morning devotion. You shared testimonies about the countless times in your life the Lord brought angels your way to assist you so you never took God or His things for granted. You influenced us with the desire to pursue a godly life and encouraged us to trust the Lord always.

You were a fierce guardian and a gentle guide who always steered us towards growth and improvement.

You had a strong belief in our potential and always encouraged us to strive for excellence. You instilled in us a drive to be the best version of ourselves. I remember when I was about to write my BECE, you noticed that I wasn't studying as I should. You sat me down and asked me about my ambitions for life. We had a long chat and you advise me that without studying, I wouldn't be able to achieve those ambitions. That was the turning point for me.

You always gave us the confidence that we could achieve anything we set our minds to do. You encouraged us to take every opportunity that came our way and make great things out of them. I wouldn't be where I am now if it wasn't for your consistent love and support. You were my biggest cheerleader.

When I was finishing uni, I discussed with you about my desire to join you in the UK. You did everything possible to make it happen. As soon as I arrived, you got me involved in serving in the church. You recruited me into the accounts team so I can replace you when you leave for Ghana. When it was time to leave after your studies, you cared so much about my wellbeing in a foreign land that you told so many people to look after me.

Your love for children was a testament to your endless compassion and desire to nurture and care for them.

You extended your love far beyond our small family. Your heart was so vast and so generous, that you never turned any child away. Your home was a haven to many. It was constantly filled with the laughter and chatter of children. Every child who crossed your path felt this warmth and longed to be around you. You gave birth to a few, but you nurtured a multitude and became a mother figure to so many.

When we visited Ghana in August 2023, it made no sense to me why we all cried when it was time to say goodbye but It does now - It was the last time we were going to see and hug you. If l knew that was the last time, I would have hugged you even more tightly.

Maa, you've left us too soon. Our hearts desire was for you to live a long life. As I look back at your accomplishments and the lives you have impacted, I can say that you lived your life to the full. I used to say to you that if I am able to achieve even a quarter of your life achievement's, I would consider myself successful. Maa, you have finished the race that was set for you by your maker. It's now time for you to rest beautifully in the shadows of the Almighty. We will strive to make you proud by continuing to build on the foundation you have laid so beautifully.

You may be gone, Maa but your spirit will forever light our paths and that of all those you empowered.

Many children will sing your praises for generations to come. I will miss our long chats especially the ones about your childhood struggles and how you rose above them all. I love you, Maa-more than words can say. You will be deeply missed, but never forgotten. May your beautiful soul rest in perfect peace till we meet again.

TRIBUTE BY SON-IN-LAW

Mama Lucy, as you are affectionately called; I encountered you about 20 years ago when I presented myself and asked to marry your daughter Hagar.

At the time you had just left London for Ghana so this meeting was not in person. However, I immediately sensed and experienced your dedication to family, pragmatism and the fact that you believed that if something has to be done, it must be done well.

The subsequent meetings and 'interviews' and background checks with Pastor Gospel, in whose care you had left your daughter, made it clear to me that you wouldn't just accept and take anyone as a son-in-law just for the sake of it. When I finally met you in Accra some months after we got married, I had a confirmation of the thoughts I had about you, and you sealed it with a warm and a gentle welcome.

You talked with me as a son, explaining and showing me how to navigate through marriage life. You made me feel at ease and comfortable. At this time my anx and nerves of meeting my mother-in-law the first time fell off. You made me feel like I were your own son and I felt comfortable with you as I would my own mum. From here on our relationship would be better described as mother and son.

This encounter with you gave me a practical understanding when my mum, siblings, uncles and other family members tell me that you are a warm-hearted person who treats them exceptionally well and make them feel special anytime they meet with you. Mama Lucy, you cared not only for me but also everyone I cared about.

When I visited your home in Sunyani, to my surprise, I saw a portrait of myself in your living room. I stood in a careful silent gaze of my portrait to make sure it was really me.

And you placed it amongst the portraits of people who are dear to your heart. I did make a gracious comment on seeing my portrait but what I did not reveal was how chaffed I was, and how surprised I was with the confidence you had in seeing my photo perhaps several times every day in your home. I thought to myself this lady might have seen something in me that I have probably not seen.

During our recent visit to Ghana, you left your busy schedule to meet us in Accra, spent some days with us, before accompanying us to Kumasi and then welcoming us to your home in Sunyani. You gave me your own private car to move around with. You introduced me to any family and friends you could find. You practically showed me everywhere I know in Sunyani.

One of the many memories of you that has stuck with me is when I got sick in Ghana and couldn't eat, You prepared for me your special potent pepper soup that took away my flu and restored my appetite. And I'm glad to say that since then I haven't had any of such symptoms again.

Your heart was so big, it had a place for all. I remember when we organised a birthday party for Josh, Priscilla and Linda. After the food was set and everyone was getting ready to enjoy, you took your car and excused everyone saying you'll be back soon. You did come back soon, but you didn't come alone. You brought with you many children, from where, nobody knew, to come to join in and enjoy the party. Everyone asked you where you had brought the children from. These children were not part of your own family but you graciously thought about them to bring some happiness to their lives. You showed us what a generous person you were. Whilst all of us were thinking about ourselves, focusing on how we were going to enjoy ourselves, you were thinking of other children you felt needed some much love and delicious food.

You were really a mother to many and a mother to all.

You truly loved God and served Him well with all dedication and diligence. This translated to the love you had for all manner of people. You inspired me and my family by your constant prayers, pieces of advice and lessons from your own life and experiences. Anytime we spoke with you, you would ask Michelle and Josh about their progress in school and encouraged them to work harder and smarter to become the great people God has made them to be. You would say this would make you proud.

Your attitude to hard work, business and excellence was a quality we will continue to cherish.

In our short stay with you, you had unconsciously inspired Josh with your passion, enthusiasm and instruction in business that he was very happy to stay behind in Ghana to continue to do business with you. Even at the airport, on our return, he pleaded with us to allow him to go back and help you with your businesses.

And when we returned to England, he will occasionally call you to ask how the business is going. You would take your time to teach and explain to him what has been going on in the business during the past few weeks.

Mama Lucy, you have left a lasting legacy. Not just in the physical but also the intangibles, in your children, family, the church and in all you encountered. This means that even though you are no more here with us. You still live on through the impact you have had in the lives of many people you influenced.

Mama Lucy, you have lived a good and a fulfilling life. All of us would have loved for you to live much longer on earth than you did. But we acknowledge the sovereignty of God and the fact that He has much better plans for you and for us so we won't argue with Him on this.

You loved and treated me like a son. I love and regard you as my mum. You will forever remain in my heart.

We have lost you, and it saddens our hearts. But heaven has gained you. It is also your gain because you are in a far better place. For this we are glad, and we hope in the promise of our Lord Jesus Christ who says we, who believe in Him and have trusted Him for eternal life will live, even though we die. This great promise is true for you, for us and all those who will believe and trust Jesus for their eternal salvation.

Maa Lucy, you have gone ahead of us. Rest well with the Lord till we meet again.

TRIBUTE BY PS. BENE ON BEHALF OF ELIM CITY

"Many women do noble things, but you surpass them all." — Proverbs 31:28-29

Maa Luu – A Faithful Steward, A Selfless Leader, A True Friend

With heavy hearts yet deep gratitude, we celebrate the life of our dear Rev. Mrs. Lucy Perby Nyarko, affectionately known as Maa Luu. She was more than a friend; she was a pillar, a confidante, a relentless worker in the vineyard of the Lord, and a woman of unwavering faith.

From the very inception of Elim City, Maa Luu was present—praying, serving, and leading. She was one of the pioneers who helped birth the vision of this church, never relenting in her commitment even when work transferred her to Bolgatanga as the Regional Director for VRA. Distance never diminished her devotion; rather, she expanded the work of the ministry by helping plant the Tamale branch of Elim City.

She was a woman who embraced all—young and old, rich and poor. No task was beneath her, no responsibility too overwhelming. Whether making high-level boardroom decisions as a board member or coordinating the daily operations of the church, she poured her heart into everything she did. As an associate to the Senior Pastor, she provided wise counsel and unwavering support, line-managing three departments and twelve ministries with diligence and grace.

Her recent role as the pastor of the Aseda (Twi) Service was yet another testament to her deep love for God's people. With a motherly heart and a firm spirit, she shepherded the congregation with passion, ensuring they grew in both faith and community.

Beyond her service, she shared a special bond with Pastor Gospel—a relationship built on mutual respect, trust, and understanding. Though she was a force to be reckoned with, one thing remained certain: she submitted to pastoral guidance with humility. Even in moments of disagreement, she would say, "Had it not been for Pastor..." or "My Papa has spoken, it is final." Her obedience and reverence for leadership were a mark of her wisdom and spiritual maturity.

My last encounter with Maa Luu in Accra, the night before I traveled to London, is a moment I will cherish forever. It was a time of warmth, laughter, and deep reflection—one that now holds even greater significance. Little did I know it would be our last conversation. She was special to me, to Pastor Gospel, and to the entire Elim City family. Her absence has created a gap that will take many to fill.

She will be dearly missed—especially at Christmas, a season she embraced with so much joy. The annual Children's Christmas Party she organized was a highlight for the little ones, who adored her warmth and generosity. Her high-spirited approach to the Carol Service, Christmas by the Fire, and every festive gathering made the season even more special. She was a pillar in the Women's Network, a cherished part of the Diamond Congregation, and an invaluable member of the Finance Team, where her sharp mind and integrity were unmatched.

For Pastor Adams and me, our Three Musketeers team is no more. The bond we shared, the moments of deep discussions, strategy meetings, and laughter—those will never be the same again.

Maa Luu's resilience in faith and service was exceptional. She didn't just preach the gospel—she lived it. Whether leading in worship, auditing church finances, or dancing joyfully in praise, she embodied the heart of a true servant of God.

Her legacy in Elim City is indelible. We have lost a gem, but heaven has gained a saint. Though she is absent in body, her impact remains. We take solace in the fact that she has fought the good fight, finished her race, and kept the faith.

Rest well and thank you Rev Mrs Perbi - Nyarko, Elim's sweet sixteen and our own Maa Luu. Your life was a testimony, and your reward is sure. Till we meet again in glory.

With love and gratitude,
Your friends, colleagues, and church family at Elim City

TRIBUTE BY CLEMENT DANSO TANDOH

Childhood friend and work colleague

It is with great sadness, yet incredible honour that I write this tribute for our sister, our colleague, our friend Rev Mrs Lucy Perbi-Nyarko.

"I am the resurrection and the life. He who believes in Me though he may die, he shall live. And whoever lives and believes in Me shall never die"
— John 11:25-26

Our dear Pastor and Sister Lucy, we are thankful to God for your life. You were so loving, caring and true to be with. As we grew up through our youthful years, we remember the fun and laughter which will always remain in our hearts. The memories we created together will last forever.

Today, family members, friends and loved ones have gathered to pay our last respect to a unique and virtuous lady Pastor Mrs Lucy Perbi Nyarko. Your tragic passing was a shock beyond imagination.

Crosby, Eugene, Rhoda and I were at the hospital when the doctor informed us that you were nearing the end of life. In that moment, my world stood still.

Death has led us to ask the doctors "what is meant by nearing the end of life" what is life truly worth? Today we find it so hard to bid you farewell to a devoted mother, diligent sister, compassionate woman and a people's person. We ask ourselves, what does farewell mean if not death. But will death itself be farewell? How profound is that tragedy that has engulfed us?

Loving Caring Devoted Diligent Compassionate Virtuous

Now, for the first time in our lives, we didn't have you, Rev Mrs Lucy to turn to. The pain we feel for you is deep.

There is a void that we struggle to fill, emptiness in our family. We shall remain closer than close. May your soul rest in peace with your maker, you have done what you came to do here on earth. Your demise came suddenly but your maker understands.
